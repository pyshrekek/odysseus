Sailing Out

My crew and I immediately set sail from Circe's island, as soon as dawn hit. My crew was ready to go, and that lovely Circe sent us our farewells. I told my shipmates about the plan to bind me to the mast so I could hear the song.

We were gallantly strolling on the seas, when the wind suddenly stopped. This was it. The plan came into action - the oarsmen leapt to their feet, struck the sail, stowed it deep in the hold and sat to the oarlocks, thrashing with polished oars, frothing the water white.[Pg. 201]
